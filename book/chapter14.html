I watched as the three lay in the sprawling grove. The long grass blowing and waving with each indecisive gust of wind—unsure to go consistently in any of the cardinal directions.

There was a part of my thoughts that questioned reality.

Was it over? Is this what they speak of when they utter the Peace of Slumber's Silence?

Am I sharing the same fate as our heroes?

Uncertainty? Is that the core of fear? Or is fear the impetuous core of uncertainty?

I am loathe to admit—I don't know.

She felt panic pounding at the gates of her emotional bastion. However, invisible hands dismissed those panicked thoughts the same someone might a buzzing fly.

Greighton laid still, unmoving—but she could feel that there was still something, life, by a frayed thread clinging to their moment.

The moment's distress penetrated her internal defenses. The very same that panic couldn't outmaneuver.

Greighton Lastlight lay in unmoving defeat.

Getting to her hands and knees, Sune began to crawl towards Grey, fingers digging into the soft almost silken Terra beneath her hands.

Before she set her hand down to inch closer, she heard a subdued yowl. Its whimpering cries were carried to her ears by the wind—like a cooperating ally this time, ensuring the pleas landed in her ears.

Her strawberry blond hair whipped over her shoulder as she saw the silhouette of…

"ZONA!" she screamed.

There was no hesitation this time. There was no prolonged dramatic crawl to uncertainty. She scurried to her feet, stumbling several times before she made her way to the crumpled Phase Lioness.

"Zona…" was all Sune could get out past her choked sobs.

The predator looked terrible. Ragged breaths pained the big cat. Her rib cage was deformed, collapsed in on itself.

An eye socket was collapsed, preventing that soothing purple glimmer from shimmering, and in the other eye—there was a dull purple.

The cat loosed a short burst of a purr, her nose nuzzling against Sune's trembling hand, as one and then another heavy huff escaped before her chest fell and failed to rise again.

The panic left.

Instead, visceral anger, rage fueled by sudden loss? The memories of soft fur, rough tongue lapping against her face, and the beast rubbing against Sune's legs.

She was marked by Zona, scented by Zona, declared to be Zona's by the beast's protective heart, and now? Now she lay before her broken, shattered, life fading away into thinning wisps of despondence that fluttered away.

"No, no, no," was the desperate entreaty to forestall the moment.

She reached out, fingers moving through the bloody and soaked fur as a sensation started at her shoulders.

It felt like a warmed blanket. The sensation tightening against her shoulders, as the emotional distress was without explanation shoved into oblivion.

"No," she declared. "No, Zona, come back to me now!"

"Now!" a final command as purple hued light began to initially pull from her body, encircling her hands.

When she laid her hands upon Zona, she acted with a conviction that her actions were proper—a destined truth.

The haze that started at her palms was now an entire shimmering and glinting purple cloud that surrounded and lifted Zona bodily from the ground.

The Song hummed through a gentle gust of wind that tousled her hair while the purple cloud, thick and undulating, began to permeate Zona's body—until the cloud had vanished.

Silence that seemed to drag forever filled her with uncertain dread.

Until both of Zona's eyes opened, and the predator reared her head back and roared with the supernatural ferocity that buoyed all of Sune, her panic, fear, uncertainty, all of it faded away as purple glowing eyes stared down to her…

Proudly.

The cat floated forward, taking a gentle and tentative step toward Sune, her ears twitching as each motion tested the healing and knitting of her wounds.

"Feel better?" Sune asked, uncertain of what had just happened.

The cat purred in reassurance.

"Sune?" a voice, warbling with trepidation, called out.

Her head snapped, the Aeris about her following her wishes, and a gust of breeze sent the long, gentle green grass bristling and undulating in the direction of the voice.

"Ranessa!"—the relief erupted like a breach in a dam.

"Sune…" Ranessa began, but she didn't give the druid time to respond.

Like wildfire, the need for safety roared through Sune.

With no hesitation, she threw herself at Ranessa. The powerful druid did not stand unbending, but instead caught Sune with a gentility that made even Zona cry out a soft mewl.

"What did I do?" Sune whimpered in Ranessa's arms. "What am I?"

"You are…" and there was no hesitation, not even the slightest moment of indecision on Ranessa's part, and Sune felt it—and the relief that washed over her. "…my friend, my little sister."

And that moment of peace—tranquil blanket wrapped in the ownership of family—caused Sune to shudder and weep.

She said nothing, but in the silence of the embrace, Sune found what she needed. It was more than personal agency. It was stronger than understanding the Song and Resonance. Her own Wellspring was ignored by one overpowering emotion.

A sense of belonging, and she belonged where she was—in Ranessa's arms.

Safe.

She let herself be held.

The peace of her environment turned time into an afterthought—if it could even be called that.

With a shaky breath, she looked up at Ranessa, and she could see her own prismatic gaze reflected in the druid's wet eyes.

"What am I, Ranessa? What kind of…"

"A blessing, child," a voice—sounding much more ancient and tired than she'd ever heard of it. "As I told you at the quarry, you are a blessing," Thabius said while shuffling closer.

She watched as the old man leaned on his staff for support. His face muddied, bloodied, and pain registered in his expression with every step.

"Thabius…" Ranessa and Sune's declarative whisper was carried on the soft warm breeze.

"What is this…" then his eyes opened wide, his head frantically shifting from left to right.

"Impossible," he said—and she could feel his fear more than hear it.

"Are you…"

"No, Druid," he said quietly—not shunning her, nor silencing her—just admitting that he wasn't okay.

Sune could figure as much out on her own.

"But I now," he grasped his staff, pulling himself gingerly to his feet. "I know our mission is a righteous one."

"Thabius, this place—tell me."

Sune looked at Ranessa who stood with the same stone-like determination that separated her from everyone and everything.

"This place…" the old man said, a hand smoothing his dishevelled long beard as he tried to regain control. "This place…" he repeated.
