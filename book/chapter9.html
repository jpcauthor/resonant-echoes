Greighton had left before Adryah could serve breakfast. She had made some concoction that involved batter and berries.

Ranessa and Gunnar seemingly had a competition to see who could eat the most, and I must admit it was close—Gunnar almost won.

Sune, however, was not present for breakfast this morning. Instead, she stood outside on the banks of the river, watching the water rush past the large island in the middle where the Velatrium stood.

She stood there stoically, with no one to bother her but her own thoughts.

I cannot say I know what she was thinking, but I can say that neither a smile nor a frown crossed her face—instead, a look of curiosity had claimed residency of the young woman.

That was until a man, about her age—at least in appearance—came to stand next to her, and after a few moments of silence, he finally said, "Good morning."

He scared her.

It took Greighton to get him out of the river after Sune inexplicably summoned the prowess of Terra to lurch and launch the young man into the rushing waters.

"Put a shirt on, Songweaver," Ranessa growled one last time and stomped away.

But it served a purpose, or at least for Sune it did.

She pulled her head from the cloud and hurriedly followed Ranessa, as she could hear Greighton berating the man while Adryah's laughter began to fade as she tried to catch up with Ranessa.

"Ranessa!" Sune hissed, trying to match her strides but failing.

With a grunt, the druid stopped and cast a mildly irritated gaze back to her.

Embarrassed, she shook her head in the negative.

"Well," Ranessa's voice grew a bit—and, surprisingly, more happy, "then your reaction is absolutely commendable and expected," Ranessa grunted, the growling druid persona having returned.

"He did have a well-honed body, I will give him that much credit," Ranessa added hurriedly. "But be careful!"

"He's a Songweaver, a singer, a bard, a troubadour, or whatever you want to call them. He leaves your coin purse lighter and your village with one or two more maidens left carrying a babe."

"Oh."

"Plus, he's an Avenen—they can appear to fit in anywhere," Ranessa said warningly, "even in a Coppice," she added with neither vitriol nor loathing, but with something more emotional.

She watched as the druid stiffened, as if realizing she had said something she ought not to have, and then began to turn around to walk back into the townhouse.

"Come, let's pack," Ranessa ordered. "I have…"

Sune didn't let her finish. "Ranessa, what's wrong? You are like a storm—calm, then torrential downpour, then sprinkles of rain, and then hail—you're giving me emotional dizziness."
